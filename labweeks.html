HCI Labweeks

In these two weeks I worked together with my group on our interactive concept in Maakhaven. After those weeks building an interactive installation, we presented it in an exhibition.

Day 1: The Startup

On the first day, we got introduced to the Lab weeks and got to know more about what we will do. Then we split up into our assigned groups and got to know each other and the specific topic that we were working with.

Wearables!

That’s the topic I did get with my four groupmates. What do we do now? Is what we thought. We were supposed to work based on the scrum method. So, we’ve made a scrum board and asked ourselves what we need to do for day one. We decided to do some brainstorming and mind mapping to come up with an idea to make. We talked about the topic wearables, so what reasons are there, what wearables exist, where can they be used for and on what areas. We talked about our hobbies, experiences with wearables and problems we have on the daily life what we maybe can use.

We researched what elements we needed if we wanted the idea to come alive. We found out that we needed, for a basic first prototype, an Arduino board, and some sort of Vibration sensor. We got those from our lecturers and started to solder one of the vibration sensors to cables of the Arduino board, so we could later on connect them with the board and therefore the code. After the soldering, we connected it to the board and with the help of some YouTube videos, Alec started coding. But even after trying multiple times, it still wouldn’t work, and we didn’t know why, so we decided to try it with another sensor, which wasn’t soldered and connected it through some jumper cables. To our excitement, this worked and we were very surprised by the strength of the vibration. Since we only had three sensors, one of which with only one cable, we decided to try the soldered one again, by removing it from the Arduino cables. Unfortunately, it still didn’t work, which for us meant that we had to order some ourselves online, to get them the following day. By the end of the day, we had ordered the sensors from TinyTronics, and sat together for a small discussion on what our next steps would be. We decided that we still had to buy some slippers, test all the motors/sensors and get the motors to be controlled by code (change its behaviour). We also needed to ask ourselves the question of how we wanted the product design to be. We ended the meeting with one of the lecturers summing up the day and telling us what we can expect from tomorrow.

We chose the subjects music and festivals, because we all have a connection with this and is fun for everyone. After going deeper on the both topics, we thought about different inputs and outputs and what people can do with it. Finally, we came up with the idea to track the rhythm of a song someone plays on their phone/laptop or another device with Wi-Fi and give impulses by vibrations in someone’s shoes e.g. insoles. In that case people can wear this in festivals, concerts, parties but also at home. Users can feel the rhythm and it gives them an extra dimension.

To make the vibration work, we had to solder a larger cable as an extension for testing and for connecting it to the Arduino board. The vibration motor can have a maximum of 3 volt and the cables had to be plugged into the ground and one in 3,5. Instead of using digitalWrite in the code we used analogWrite, so that we didn’t had to use the Arduino board but just doing it analogue. The first motor we had just didn’t work what took us some time. We think it was broken because of the soldering. Another one worked with jumper cables. The code we’ve used didn’t influence the vibration. For testing and our end product we need more vibration motors so people can feel it in their whole foot/feet, which we ordered at tiny tronics.

After the guest lecture we worked on the motors and the logo. We thought it is a good idea to make different logos and combinate as far as it can. The slipper you can see in the final logo had three different colours, and the colours are shown on the places where the vibration motors can be found.



During working on the vibration motors (three in one) we found out there was not enough volt what the Arduino board can handle. The Arduino board can handle five volts in total, but each motor needs three. So, we bought a battery with nine volt. The problem is solved, and it worked.

Later on we heard that the vibration motors works as a kind of dynamo and leads energy, even when we put them off they turn further. That’s why the stream of energy goes against the direction of the normal circuit and is harmful for the Arduino Board. With a diode the stream will just go one way, in that way it will only go to the ground and not back into the Arduino board. We needed also a transistor which can be used as a sort of switch and it works on volts. If a high voltage comes in, it will go open and when the voltage goes down it will close. Another new material we will use is a capacitor that works like a buffer for the energy.

Our product: Fliphop

Our concept revolves around the feel-good festival experience. In order for you to share those amazing memories with your friends, the slippers will help you to never lose them completely. The idea is to bring people together with a proximity device that vibrates stronger or softer depending on the distance between you and your friend. The LED lights and vibration motors will tell you how far away you are from your friends.


The flip flops are equipped with LED lights, vibrating motors and a location based system. The two available settings are “find my friends” or simply “off”. We use a Bluetooth board as the location based system, this will help you to find out how close you are to your friend, by indicating the proximity through LED lights and the frequency of vibrating sensors. The vibrating motors will help to determine the location by vibrating faster without having to look down, while the LED lights will flash faster if you get closer. For a real final product, we want to recplace the Bluetooth board for a WIFI board for a greater distance and make the design without seeing all the technical stuff.

Reflection

During the course before the lab weeks, I made up some goals and expectations about the lab weeks which you road on my homepage, did I achieve these yes or no?


“I expect the two working weeks will be very intense and maybe my brain will explode afterwards, but I think it will be worth it.”

This was true, it was very intense but I loved to see the results and the progress we made in such a fast time. I think this scrum technique is an amazing way to get results real fast without making the actual prototype. My brain is full now of new technical possibilities and solutions and working techniques.


“I hope to let my creative mind work, combined with the inspiration that technology offers within this project to come up with an interactive concept. I will create a detailed concept for a valuable, innovative interaction.”

I loved that I could put my creativity in this project and use it. The skills and technology limited a little, we had big ideas which we needed to shrink a little. We can say here that I accomplished those goals and expectations, now let’s talk more about my experiences and difficult situations during those two weeks.


One of the best things is that I worked in an international team (with a German girl and a Canadian guy). Not only was this my chance to improve my English in this field of expertise, but I also get to know how people from other countries work. This was a good opportunity to prepare myself for my exchange starting from August 2019.


Something I struggled with, was my skillset. We couldn’t make some things true, because we didn’t know how to do it. I am talking about the technical part, e.g. working with Arduino. And of course, this isn’t a problem and we learned some helpful things among the way, but we needed to do this in such a short time and for those things you really need to sit down and take your time. But when we accomplished an obstacle, it was an amazing feeling. I was for example super happy when I made the LED lights work. I am not a pro in Arduino, but wherever I could, I tried to help. And when you see then the right result is just great.


One thing I recognized I do more often, is I ask in projects: ‘where is everyone/you working on?’. I think I do it to have a certain control on the project and keep an eye on the process that’s made. I don’t think it’s a bad thing, because when we were doing the daily stand-up every day, I could say what we all did and what we should do.


We had to create choices at the beginning (diverge) and through the process we made choices (converge). Sometimes I felt disappointed about us not accomplishing the ideas what we had in mind, that we had to make sacrifices. But we did set up real big goals and we couldn’t prevent this to happen. And sometimes the ideas we had in the beginning, other groups had it too. But we wanted to be original and then we changed it a little bit. For example, we wanted to learn people how to stick with the rhythm on festivals, but when we heard another group working with BPM and rhythm things, we directly stopped with working it out. Maybe next time I want to think again and I think now that it isn’t about originality but what you make out of it.


The next time I will test as much (or more if possible) as we did. We tested the lo-fi prototype quite early. In this case there was possibility to fail quickly, iterate and test again. This process we did a couple of times within our group, but also with outside test people.


If I look back, I am amazed what we accomplished in only two weeks. I was motivated and had a drive during the weeks, pretty consistent with some downs during making sacrifices. We came up with a pretty good idea which I see happening in the far future. I am content with it and maybe we can't check everything we had in mind, it still is amazing what it can do. Next time I want to make a better-looking prototype, to make it look more real for people, although at the exposition people already liked it. The slippers weren't really comfortable with all the wires going everywhere.
