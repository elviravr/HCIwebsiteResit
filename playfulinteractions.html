Playful Interactions.

Most interfaces are individual, say a laptop where you need a mouse and screen. Playful interfaces are interactive with surroundings and you can play with it together. The interfaces make use of input technology: Computer vision and sensors and output technology: Sight, touch, hear, smell and taste. For example there are piano stairs to motivate people to use the stairs instead of the elevator / escalators and motivate more people to throw rubbish in the bin by making it fun and playfull.

My idea

The chairs in the rooms of school have build-in sensors which detects if someone’s sitting on the chair or not. In the room near the projector the teacher fills in the amount of student she expects to be present in that class. Both of these inputs are the technologies behind this idea.

How it works

When someone is sitting down, there will be a cool, short sound. If there are only five students to go, there will be a count-down. And finally when the amount of students the teacher filled in is achieved, there will be played a song (e.g. one minute of the favourite song of the teacher) and the lesson can start. For every student that is on time, the group will get two points. When someone is too late, it will cost three points. When a person is still missing while the lesson has started and doensn’t come (and haven’t inform the teacher), then the total will be minus five per student.

How they can win

To know if the students will get a price, the next formula have to be calculated: [total points collected by the group of students for all the lessons of the whole period : amount of students x 100%], and when they have reached above 95%; the students win a studytrip of their choice in the Netherlands. The teachers will keep the score.

Gameplay

This is a multiplayer game and is cooperative. Everybody will be stimulated to be there on time, so it has benefits for the teacher as well for the students. The gameplay is knowing and comparing, because the players are aware of each others actions and will compare themselves to other students or classes.

Research

Project Gravity Ball

Project Gravity Ball is an interactive multiplayer game where players need to balance a physical platform to control a ball. The prototype uses camera tracking to register the movement of the ball and triggers the top-down projection onto the platform surface.

Purpose of the interface

Trying to get the ball in as many energy fields as possible before the time runs out.

Technology

Input technology: Camera tracking to register the movement of the ball.
Output technology: Triggers the top-down projection onto the platform surface.

Type of playmodus

Multiplayer, cooperative and parallel knowing.

Breakout for Two

Breakout for two is a cross between soccer, tennis, and the popular computer game "Breakout", which can be played by two players over a distance. It is a extertion interface.

Purpose of the interface

Trying to get rid of the semi-transparent blocks in the game just like you do with the arcade game Breakout to win. The users will be active physically by throwing or kicking the ball on the blocks to break them, but also have social interaction with strangers or friends.

Technology

Input technology: Camera tracking to register the movement of the ball.
Output technology: The life-size projection of the remote player and the speakers.

Type of playmodus

Multiplayer, cooperative and parallel knowing.
